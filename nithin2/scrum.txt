SCRUM THEORY
 Scrum is founded based on observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic. Scrum gives insights to optimize predictability and control risk.
Three pillars of this process are 
 a.Transperancy
 b.Inspection
 c.Adaptation
Scrum events
  .Sprint Planning
  .Daily Scrum
  .Sprint Review
  .Sprint Retrospective

SCRUM VALUES
  Commitment, courage, focus, openness and respect. Successful use of scrum depends on people becoming more proficient in living these five values.

THE PRODUCT OWNER
  Responsible for maximizing the value of the product. He is the sole person responsible for product backlog.

THE SCRUM MASTER
  He acts as the bridge between the product owner and scrum team. He resolves the issues arised between product owner and scrum team.
